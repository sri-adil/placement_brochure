ACM ICPC

IIIT-Bangalore’s team 'Mochizuki' has qualified for the World Finals of ACM-ICPC – the oldest, largest, and most prestigious programming contest in the world.

Computing disciplines at almost 2,736 universities from over 102 countries on six continents participate in this competition. With 46,381 contestants from 2,948 universities in 103 countries competed in regional competitions at over 530 sites worldwide, as part of the ACM programming contest, our studnets secured the 101th position.

This was IIITB's second appearance at the World Finals after their debut in 2017. IIIT-B Team secured the 3rd position in INDIA-FINALS and qualified to represent IIIT-Bangalore in the ACM ICPC World Finals – 2018.

In 2019, a new record was set for IIIT Bangalore in ACM ICPC as 6 teams qualify for the regionals. This is the first time IIIT Bangalore's students have qualified for all the regionals and were able to get 9 slots at the ICPC regionals. A total of 65 teams registered for the regionals.
